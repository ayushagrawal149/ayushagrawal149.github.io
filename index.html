1. Formation control of differential-drive robots with input saturation and constraints on formation size

Robot Programming and Controls Intern @ ARTPark, Bangalore, India
Dr. Mukunda Bharatheesha and Prof. Shishir Kolathaya
Accepted for presentation at 62nd IEEE CDC 2023, TechRxiv Preprint 

Proposed a novel nonlinear output-feedback trajectory tracking control which guarantees stable tracking of reference trajectory for differential-drive robots while obeying velocity limits. Analytically proved the system's stability under the controller's action using Lyapunov stability analysis. Demonstrated the the controller's utility in multi-robot formation control, and proposed constraints on formation size to ensure stability in cornering manoeuvres. 

62nd IEEE CDC 2023 Acceptance Letter
2. Active Ride Functionality ©


Vehicle Control Engineer @ Jaguar Land Rover Pvt. Ltd., India
Department of Chassis and Motion Control Systems

Designed a controller for JLR’s active suspension technology adept at boosting ride & handling by eliminating body’s motion due to road undulations, cornering, and braking. The proposed controller enhanced the primary ride quality by 31% in the New Range Rover (L460) vehicle model. Tuned the controller for optimal performance in presence of noise, network delay and actuator dynamics. Further, simulated pre-emptive suspension system using real-time control with road profile scanners, reported >90% improvement in both primary and secondary ride quality.

3. Torque Split for Efficiency ©

Mechanical Engineering Graduate Trainee @ Jaguar Land Rover, India
Department of Engineering Powertrain Systems and Controls

Reduced powertrain energy consumption by enhancing a functionality named "Torque Split for Efficiency" in JLR vehicles. Formulated an optimization routine by leveraging the interplay of heat flux management system (HFMS) and electric drive units (EDU) to reduce the net energy consumed by EDUs (front and rear) and cooling components (fans, vanes, and coolant pumps). Department of Structural Integration

5. Reduced order controller for leader-follower consensus of Multi-agent system 

Undergraduate Thesis  
Prof. Vivek Natarajan, IIT Bombay

Utilized the technique of simultaneous stabilization to derive sufficient conditions which guarantee output feedback state consensus of nth order linear agents in time-invariant communication topology. Discovered the intrinsic potential of frequency-domain based controllers in ensuring state consensus of MAS in the presence of known, uniform and arbitrarily large communication and input delays in the network. Simulated the consensus trajectory for a system of N = 5 agents with time-invariant network topology consisting of 4th order dynamics.

Project Report 
6. Seismic response of vertical post-tensioned connection for modular steel structures

MITACS Globalink Research Intern @ University of Toronto
Prof. Oya Mercan, Dept. Civil and Mineral Engineering

Performed seismic analysis of conventional steel structures with welded joints under quasi-static cyclic load (ASCI protocol) using FE model to validate the experimental force displacement behavior of the structure through numerical Skeleton curve. Further simulated the proposed vertical post-tensioned connection for modular steel building (MSB) in ABAQUS to authenticate its self centering properties and 13% higher energy dissipation capabilities compared to welded connections.

Project Report 
7. MAPLE based solver for stress analysis of a loaded elastic cavity 

Summer Research Intern @ Indian Institute of Technology Bombay
Prof. Tanmay Bhandakkar, Dept. of Mechanical Engineering

Developed a solver in MAPLE employing general Michell solution to obtain the solution field for two dimensional infinite annular domain with generalised internal traction boundary condition. Simulated the problem in ABAQUS using a symmetric model for continuous, piecewise continuous, point load distribution to verify the results obtained through numerical model in MAPLE. Explored and verified Saint Venant’s Principle for statically equivalent uniform & linear piecewise continuous load distribution by analyzing the stress distribution using MATLAB plots. 

Project report 
8. Image Creating Robotic Arm

Team Leader, Institute Technical Summer Project (ITSP)
Institute Technical Council, IIT Bombay | Certificate of Completion

Designed the structure of RR planar manipulator using U-shaped Aluminum brackets, and performed FEM simulations in ABAQUS to determine the requisite torque capacity of the servo motors. Developed an algorithm for the movement of actuators supporting the arm by utilizing the bit-Matrix obtained by processing the Canny Edge Detected image on MATLAB. Determined the joint space trajectory using Inverse Dynamics and programmed Arduino UNO micro controller to trace the edges of the image in the task space through servo actuators.  

Project Report 
9. Control Design for ABB-IRB 1600 - A 6DoF Industrial Robotic Manipulator

Semester-Long Course Project for ME 604 Robotics
Prof. Abhishek Gupta, IIT Bombay

Modelled the manipulator dynamics using MATLAB Robotics Toolbox and implemented PD controller with gravity compensation to track the joint space trajectory planned using quintic polynomial. Minimized steady-state error of 1.8% in Independent Joint Control by introducing Joint Space Inverse Dynamics Controller to achieve 99.8% accuracy in set point tracking and path planning problem.
