Formation control of differential-drive robots with input saturation and constraints on formation size

Robot Programming and Controls Intern @ ARTPark, Bangalore, India
Dr. Mukunda Bharatheesha and Prof. Shishir Kolathaya
Accepted for presentation at 62nd IEEE CDC 2023, TechRxiv Preprint 

Proposed a novel nonlinear output-feedback trajectory tracking control which guarantees stable tracking of reference trajectory for differential-drive robots while obeying velocity limits. Seismic response of vertical post-tensioned connection for modular steel structures

MITACS Globalink Research Intern @ University of Toronto
Prof. Oya Mercan, Dept. Civil and Mineral Engineering

Performed seismic analysis of conventional steel structures with welded joints under quasi-static cyclic load (ASCI protocol) using FE model to validate the experimental force displacement behavior of the structure through numerical Skeleton curve. Further simulated the proposed vertical post-tensioned connection for modular steel building (MSB) in ABAQUS to authenticate its self centering properties and 13% higher energy dissipation capabilities compared to welded connections.

Project Report 
MAPLE based solver for stress analysis of a loaded elastic cavity 

Summer Research Intern @ Indian Institute of Technology Bombay
Prof. Tanmay Bhandakkar, Dept. of Mechanical Engineering

Developed a solver in MAPLE employing general Michell solution to obtain the solution field for two dimensional infinite annular domain with generalised internal traction boundary condition. Simulated the problem in ABAQUS using a symmetric model for continuous, piecewise continuous, point load distribution to verify the results obtained through numerical model in MAPLE. Explored and verified Saint Venant’s Principle for statically equivalent uniform & linear piecewise continuous load distribution by analyzing the stress distribution using MATLAB plots.

Image Creating Robotic Arm

Team Leader, Institute Technical Summer Project (ITSP)
Institute Technical Council, IIT Bombay

Designed the structure of RR planar manipulator using U-shaped Aluminium brackets, and performed FEM simulations in ABAQUS to determine the requisite torque capacity of the servo motors. Developed an algorithm for the movement of actuators supporting the arm by utilizing the bit-Matrix obtained by processing the Canny Edge Detected image on MATLAB. Determined the joint space trajectory using Inverse Dynamics and programmed Arduino UNO micro controller to trace the edges of the image in the task space through servo actuators.

Control Design of ABB-IRB 1600 - 6DoF Robotic Manipulator

Course Project for ME 604 Robotics
Prof. Abhishek Gupta, Dept. of Mechanical Engineering

Modelled the manipulator dynamics using MATLAB Robotics Toolbox and implemented PD controller with gravity compensation to track the joint space trajectory planned using quintic polynomial. Minimized steady-state error of 1.8% in Independent Joint Control by introducing Joint Space Inverse Dynamics Controller to achieve 100% accuracy in set point tracking and path planning problem.
